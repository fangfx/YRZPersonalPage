Resources for Students

This page provides some interesting links that I personally used and found very helpful. If you find a broken link, please report to me at zhengyr at mst.edu

Career Development

  • Advice for Undergraduates Considering Graduate School by Dr. Phil Agre of UCLA
  • Networking on the Network: A Guide to Professional Skills for PhD Students also by Dr. Phil Agre of UCLA [ PDF ]
  • How to Organize your Thesis by Dr. John W. Chinneck of Carleton University

Writing Scientific Papers

  • Writing Technical Articles by Dr. Henning Schulzrinne of Columbia University. It's a must read before you start writing and during your writing. Provides excellent details on good approaches, common mistakes, and LaTex. Excellent reference list to other links on technical writing. Also has hints for Ph.D. proposal and thesis defense
  • Writing a Math Phase Two Paper by Dr. STEVEN L. KLEIMAN of MIT. Provides excellent guidelines on paper organization, concise writing, and English grammar involving math expressions. [ PDF ]
  • Three Sins of Authors in Computer Science and Math by Dr. Jonathan Shewchuk of UC Berkeley.
  • W. Strunk, Jr. and E.B. White, The Elements of Style, MacMillan Publishing Co., New York, NY, 1959. Web version, by W. Strunk, Jr.
  • English grammar questions by Dr. Grammar (a.k.a. Jim HiDuke), a former English professor at the University of Northern Iowa. You may want to check this out even if you are a native English speaker.

LaTeX, IEEEtran.cls and related

  • LATEX Demonstrations by Dr. Harvey Greenberg of CU Denver [ PS ]
  • The Not So Short Introduction to LaTeX 2e by Tobias Oetiker et al. [ PDF ]
  • IEEEtran.cls from CTAN The IEEE style templates and user manuals for papers submitting to IEEE journals and conferences.
  • IEEE Information for Authors Manuscript preparation procedures for submittng to IEEE journals.
  • XFIG The free software for drawing diagrams. To be used on Unix and Linux machines.
  • The Collection of Computer Science Bibliographies . This is a BibTeX database containing more than 2 million references.

Polishing Presentation Skills

  • Some Lecturing Heuristics based on Dr. Patrick H. Winsoton's lecture at MIT.
  • Giving a talk by Dr. Frank Kschischang at University of Toronto [ PDF ]
  • How to give a talk by Dr. Bruce Randall Donald
